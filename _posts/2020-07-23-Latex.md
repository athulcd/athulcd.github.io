If you are in college, specially in math based program, you will end up using Latex at some point or other. You may end up using it for writing your essays, homeworks, assignments, research papers etc. Although it may seem intimidating to a first time user, latex can be mastered via practice.\\
While there are a plethora of resources online like []() or [](), I believe that learning by doing is a much better alternative. I recommend using an online platform to get started. [Overleaf](https://www.overleaf.com?r=c0f0013c&rm=d&rs=b) is probably the most popular one out there and is widely used for collaborative projects in academia. Create a free overleaf account using this [link](https://www.overleaf.com?r=c0f0013c&rm=d&rs=b). After creating an account I recommend reading this [Learn LaTeX in 30 minutes](https://www.overleaf.com/learn/latex/Learn_LaTeX_in_30_minutes) by Overleaf. They also provides a good [documentation](https://www.overleaf.com/learn/latex/Main_Page) and [tutorials](https://www.overleaf.com/learn/latex/Tutorials). But I would recommend you to try and replicate something you have encountered in real life - a math problem set or exam paper or even your favourite theorem. Don't hesitate to google whenever you feel you are stuck. It is going to take some time before you memorize all the basic commands. Till then google is your best friend. [Tex Stack Exchange](https://tex.stackexchange.com/) will also handy if you are facing a problem. More often than not someone has already posted the same query a few years ago.\\
Once you are comfortable online, I recommend that you move to an offline platform. At some point in future you will be forced to do this. Other than the ability to work in the absence of internet, it provides a lot of features available in a modern text editor.
