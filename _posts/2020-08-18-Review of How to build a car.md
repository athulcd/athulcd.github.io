<a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/35657708-how-to-build-a-car" style="float: left; padding-right: 20px"><img border="0" alt="How to Build a Car: The Autobiography of the World’s Greatest Formula 1 Designer" src="https://i.gr-assets.com/images/S/compressed.photo.goodreads.com/books/1509876857l/35657708._SX98_.jpg" /></a><a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/35657708-how-to-build-a-car">How to Build a Car: The Autobiography of the World’s Greatest Formula 1 Designer</a> by <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/3955153.Adrian_Newey">Adrian Newey</a><br/>
My rating: <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/3482114092">5 of 5 stars</a><br /><br />
A beautiful book which provides insights into the making of some of the most successful F1 cars. An excellent read for anyone interested in F1/motor sports.<br /><br />The book features anecdotes involving legends like Mika Hakkinen, Alain Prost, Nigel Mansell, David Coulthard, Damon Hill, Jacques Villeneuve, Michael Schumacher, Sebastian Vettel, Lewis Hamilton and Ayrton Senna.<br /><br />Newey shares his role in the iconic rivalries which defined the sport in the 90s and 00s like Adrian Newey vs Ross Brawn, McLaren vs Williams vs Ferrari. Frank Williams, Ron Dennis, Bernie Eccleston, Max Mosley and other big men in F1 history are also part of this.<br /><br />Ingenuity is a recurring theme in the book. The length which people go to interpret rules and regulations to find loopholes and exploit them is astonishing. Another recurring idea is the trade-off between building faster cars and ensuring safety. There's an entire chapter about his fatal accident in a Williams car designed by Newey. <br /><br />The technical details might appear difficult for someone new to the sport/without a background in engineering. But that will not prevent you from enjoying the book.
